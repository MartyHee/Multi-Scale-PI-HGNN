## F5: Physics Regularization Ablation

**Status:** ✅ Paper-ready (regenerated after Phase 3-precheck)
**Priority:** High
**Type:** Dual-panel bar chart (matplotlib)

### Content
- **Left panel:** "BC Constraint Satisfaction"
  - 4 bars: MS-HGT gated, BC-only, Link-only, Full
  - y-axis: Constrained DOF MAE (m)
  - % change annotations: -22.3% (BC), +14.5% (Link), -38.8% (Full)
- **Right panel:** "Force Tail Error"
  - Same 4 models
  - y-axis: Force P95 Absolute Error (N/N·m)
  - Value annotations on bars

### Source data
```
MS-HGT gated:   BC DOF MAE 0.000242, Force P95 AE 38,000
MS-PI-HGT-BC:   BC DOF MAE 0.000188, Force P95 AE 39,756
MS-PI-HGT-Link: BC DOF MAE 0.000277, Force P95 AE 39,277
MS-PI-HGT-Full: BC DOF MAE 0.000148, Force P95 AE 37,917
```

### Caption draft
> **Figure 5.** Physics Regularization Ablation. Dual-panel bar chart comparing four physics loss variants. Left: Support boundary condition satisfaction — Full variant reduces constrained DOF MAE by 38.8% relative to the baseline (MS-HGT gated without physics loss). Right: Force tail error — Full variant achieves the lowest P95 absolute error among all variants. The BC-only variant improves constraint satisfaction but shows limited tail error reduction. The Link-only variant does not improve constraint satisfaction. These results demonstrate that physics-informed regularization improves physical consistency without degrading predictive accuracy.


### Caption draft
> **Figure 6.** Conformal Uncertainty Quantification for MS-PI-HGT-Full on the test_graph_50_50 split. Panel 1: Per-DOF displacement coverage at α=0.10. All 6 DOF achieve near-nominal coverage (all within ±0.5pp of 90%). Panel 2: Corresponding prediction interval half-widths on a logarithmic scale showing wide dynamic range (2.1e-5 rad to 2.0e-3 m). Panel 3: Region joint 6-DOF coverage — this is a diagnostic observation, not a conformal marginal guarantee. Joint coverage is substantially lower than marginal coverage, reflecting the stricter simultaneous coverage requirement when all 6 DOF must fall within their intervals concurrently.
